# Ask A Manager Salary Survey Analysis (Python)
## Project Overview
This project analyzes the Ask A Manager Salary Survey dataset to uncover key insights into salary trends across industries, experience levels, locations, gender, education, and other demographic factors. The analysis focuses on identifying the key drivers of salary differences and providing data-driven insights to support career and compensation decisions.

## Objectives
- Identify the highest paying industries
- Analyze salary growth based on years of experience
- Examine geographical variations in salary
- Investigate gender-based salary differences
- Evaluate the impact of education level on salary
- Explore the relationship between total experience and field-specific experience

## Tools & Technologies
- Python
- Pandas
- Matplotlib
- Seaborn
- Jupyter Notebook
- GitHub

## Dataset
### Source: Ask A Manager Salary Survey
### Description: The dataset contains over 28,000 salary records across multiple industries, job roles, experience levels, and demographic groups.
### Key Features
- Industry
- Job Title
- Annual Salary
- Work Country
- Years of Professional Experience
- Years of Experience in Field
- Education Level
- Gender
- Race
### Data Cleaning Performed
- Standardized country names
- Converted salary column to numeric format
- Handled missing values
- Cleaned inconsistent categorical entries
- Removed irrelevant and invalid entries

## Key Analysis Questions
- Which industries pay the highest salaries?
- How does salary change with experience?
- How does location affect salary levels?
- Is there a gender pay gap?
- How does education level influence salary?
- What is the relationship between total experience and field-specific experience?

## Key Insights
- Tech, Finance, and Engineering industries have the highest average salaries
- Salary increases consistently with years of experience
- The United States has the highest salary averages across most roles
- Higher education levels correlate with higher salaries
- A gender pay gap exists, with males having slightly higher average salaries
- Field-specific experience has a stronger impact on salary than total experience

## Recommendations
- Professionals should consider high-paying industries like Tech and Finance
- Developing specialized skills increases earning potential
- Employers should implement equitable compensation practices
- Continuous learning and advanced education improve salary growth
- Companies should use salary benchmarking to remain competitive
